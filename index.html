What is area?Area is the amount of flat space inside a shape — measured in square units like cm², m², or ft²!
What is Area?
Understanding Area 📐
Area = the space inside a 2D shape, measured in square units
Think of it this way
Counting Squares
Imagine your shape on graph paper. Count every little square inside — that's the area! Each square = 1 square unit.
Real-world examples
We use area every day!
🏠 Flooring a room
🌱 Measuring a garden

Key idea!Area is always in SQUARE units — cm², m², in². The little ² means "squared" = two dimensions: length and width!
Square
Area of a Square 🟦
All 4 sides are equal — so we just multiply the side by itself!
The Formula
Area = s × s = s²
s = the length of one side
Since all sides are equal, just multiply side × side!
1
Measure one side (all sides equal)
Example: side = 6 cm
2
Multiply: side × side
6 × 6 = 36
3
Write the answer in square units
Area = 36 cm²

Real-world!A square room is 5 m on each side. Area = 5 × 5 = 25 m². You'd need 25 floor tiles (each 1m²) to cover it!
Rectangle
Area of a Rectangle 🟥
Two different side lengths — multiply length by width!
The Formula
Area = l × w
l = length (longer side)
w = width (shorter side)
Multiply them together to get the area!
1
Find the length and width
Length = 8 cm, Width = 4 cm
2
Multiply: length × width
8 × 4 = 32
3
Add square units to your answer
Area = 32 cm²
Interactive Rectangle Builder

🎨
Real-world!A painting is 90 cm long and 60 cm wide. Area = 90 × 60 = 5,400 cm². That's how much canvas the artist used!
Triangle
Area of a Triangle 🔺
A triangle is exactly half of a rectangle — so we use half the formula!
The Formula
Area = ½ × b × h
b = base (bottom side)
h = height (straight up, not slanted!)
Multiply base × height, then halve it!
1
Find the base and the height
Base = 10 cm, Height = 6 cm
2
Multiply: base × height
10 × 6 = 60
3
Divide by 2 (that's the "half"!)
60 ÷ 2 = 30 cm²
Interactive Triangle Builder

cm² (square centimetres)
🏔️
Why half?Cut ANY rectangle diagonally — you get two identical triangles. So a triangle is always exactly half the rectangle that surrounds it!
